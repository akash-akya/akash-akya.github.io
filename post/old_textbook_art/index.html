Art in old textbook

I recently got into digging old Kannada books from archive.org. By old I mean long before independence. Some of them are dated 1865! While the language used in the book sounds raw or weird sometimes as the usage of words or sentences are little different from the current accepted “standard Kannada”. But make no mistake, it’s not a difficult language to understand! On the contrary, I think more people might understand this language than the current one. Part of it might be because of lesser usage of Sanskrit words. Of course not all of them use the same dialect. You can even guess the region of the dialect used since its very similar current dialects.

Language is only part of the reason I got interested. A book is essentially a window to different time-space. We get to see a different set of beliefs, morals and social norms. Sometimes these can vary from being off-beat/weird to offensive from today’s standard. And yet some of it is still very same and thus whole thing look so familiar. I wonder what will the generation after 150 years will think of us and our current norms. Anyway I’ll rant more about these things some other time. For now let’s see these photo-realistic arts I found in a first standard textbook published in 1906.

The posing, composition and the detail suggest that these are based off of photographs. But these are not halftone prints as one might expect. Rather these are a created from lines pattern of varying lengths and width, producing much finer detailed print. Perhaps a skilled artist created these by tracing original photograph. Also, the print looks very clean. I couldn’t find any artifacts.
