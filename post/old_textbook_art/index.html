Art in old textbook

May 6, 2021

I recently got into digging old Kannada books from archive.org. By old I mean long before 1947. Some of them are dated 1865! While the language used in the book sounds raw or of putting when compared to the current standard Kannada we use in publishing. But make no mistake, it’s not difficult to understand! On the contrary, I think more people might understand this language than the current standard one. Part of it might be because of the lesser usage of Sanskrit words which is more in the current standard. Since there is no standard Kannada each book uses a different dialect. You can even guess the region of the dialect used since it’s very similar to current regional dialects.

Language is only one reason I got interested. A book is essentially a window to different time-space. We get to see the world from different glasses. Sometimes their worldview can be offensive from today’s standard. And sometimes it might look so familiar. I wonder what will the generation after 150 years will think of us and our current world view. Anyway, I’ll rant more about this some other time. For now, let’s see these photo-realistic arts I found in a first standard textbook published in 1906.

The posing, composition, and details suggest that these are based on photographs. But these are not halftone prints as one might expect. Rather these are created from lines pattern of varying lengths and width, producing a much finer detailed print. My best guess is its created using Stipple engraving technique. A skilled artist might have created the engraving these by tracing the original photograph. What makes it even more interesting is that the effort they put into the first standard textbook in 1906 and for very few pupils. The quality of the print is no less than modern books. From the scan, we can clearly see that the print is very clean. I couldn’t find any artifacts.
